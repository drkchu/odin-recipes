Chocolate Macarons

Description:

Chocolate macarons are a delightful and decadent dessert that have their roots in France. These delicate treats consist of two small, round cookies made from ground almonds, egg whites, and sugar, with a filling of rich chocolate ganache sandwiched between them. Chocolate macarons are a beloved dessert in France, and have gained popularity around the world in recent years. They are perfect for special occasions, as a gift, or simply as a delicious indulgence. Whether you enjoy them as a snack or as part of a dessert spread, chocolate macarons are a must-try for any lover of sweets.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 3 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 3 egg whites, room temperature
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 6 oz. dark chocolate, chopped

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, sift together the powdered sugar, almond flour, and cocoa powder. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat the egg whites with an electric mixer on medium speed until frothy. Add the cream of tartar and continue to beat until soft peaks form.
  4. Gradually add the granulated sugar to the egg whites, one tablespoon at a time, while continuing to beat on high speed. Beat until stiff peaks form and the mixture is shiny and glossy.
  5. Using a rubber spatula, fold the almond flour mixture into the egg whites in three batches, gently stirring until the mixture is smooth and forms a ribbon when lifted.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Pipe small circles of batter onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each one.
  7. Tap the baking sheet gently on the counter to release any air bubbles, then let the macarons rest at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until a thin film forms on the surface.
  8. Bake the macarons for 15-18 minutes, until they are set and the tops are firm to the touch. Let them cool completely on the baking sheet.
  9. Meanwhile, make the chocolate ganache filling. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it just begins to simmer.
  10. Remove the cream from the heat and pour it over the chopped chocolate in a medium bowl. Let the mixture sit for a minute or two, then stir until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth.
  11. Chill the ganache in the refrigerator for about 15 minutes, until it thickens and is easy to spread.
  12. Once the macarons are cool, pair them up by size and spread a small amount of the ganache on the flat side of one macaron. Sandwich the two halves together, pressing gently to distribute the filling evenly.
  13. Chill the macarons in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.
